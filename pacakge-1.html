Goldern Package

Embark on a captivating 9-day family adventure through Sri Lanka, packed with diverse activities and breathtaking sights. Begin in Colombo with a city tour, then visit the Pinnawala Elephant Orphanage. Explore the ancient Dambulla Cave Temples and enjoy a safari and climb at Sigiriya Rock Fortress. Discover the ruins of Polonnaruwa and visit spice villages. Experience cultural highlights in Matale and Kandy, including the Temple of the Tooth Relic. Wander through the Royal Botanic Garden in Peradeniya, tour the picturesque Nuwara Eliya, and take a scenic train ride to Ella. Conclude with a visit to a turtle hatchery before returning to Colombo.

Glimpse of your experience

Pinnawala Elephant Orphanage

Visiting the 25-acre coconut property of Pinnawala Elephant Orphanage offers a heartwarming experience. Witness baby elephants being bottle-fed and roaming freely without posing any threat. Since 1975, this sanctuary has cared for these gentle giants, some orphaned or disabled. Since its inception, the orphanage has successfully bred and raised over 100 elephants, marking it as one of the world’s largest captive breeding programs for elephants. . Don't miss the feeding sessions at 09:15 am, 12:15 pm, and 05:00 pm, or their twice-daily river baths at 10:00 am and 02:00 pm. It's an once in a life time unforgettable encounter, unique to this sanctuary.

Sigiriya rock fortress

Sigiriya, a fifth-century rock citadel and UNESCO World Heritage Site, epitomizes ancient Sri Lankan engineering and urban planning. It's celebrated not just for its architectural prowess but also as a cultural masterpiece. The site, accessed through the iconic 'Lion Paws,' overlooks meticulously crafted royal gardens, showcasing the fortress's former grandeur. Surrounded by ramparts and moats, Sigiriya is likened to a mythical 'City of Gods' and adorned with frescoes resembling Gupta art from India's Ajanta caves. The western 'mirror wall,' covered in ancient graffiti, vividly illustrates Sigiriya's rich artistic heritage.

Nine Arch Bridge

The Nine Arch Bridge, also known as the "Bridge in the Sky," is a famous destination in Sri Lanka, commissioned by the British in 1921. It stands as a remarkable example of colonial-era railway construction, showcasing the engineering prowess of that period. Built during British rule in Sri Lanka from 1815 to 1948, the bridge's construction highlights the innovative techniques and architectural achievements of the British Empire in the region.
