Glimpse of your experience

Nilaveli Beach

Nilaveli Beach, located on Sri Lanka's northeast coast near Trincomalee, is celebrated for its pristine white sandy beaches and clear turquoise waters, drawing visitors from both near and far. The beach offers a serene and tranquil atmosphere ideal for relaxation and a variety of water activities such as swimming, snorkeling, and diving. It is also renowned for dolphin and whale watching, particularly during specific seasons when these marine creatures migrate through the area. Adjacent to Nilaveli Beach is Pigeon Island National Park, accessible via a short boat ride, where visitors can explore coral reefs and encounter diverse marine life while snorkeling.

Koneshwaram Kovil

Koneswaram Kovil, also referred to as Konesar Temple or Thirukoneswaram Kovil, is a Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva. Positioned on Swami Rock, a prominent cliff overlooking the Indian Ocean in Trincomalee, Sri Lanka, this temple stands as a testament to both historical and religious significance dating back to ancient times. It is revered as a sacred site among Hindus and is known for its stunning location and architectural splendor.

Madu Ganga

Madu Ganga is a complex network of lagoons, mangrove swamps, and islands spanning over 900 hectares. It is recognized as one of the last remaining pristine mangrove forests in Sri Lanka, teeming with diverse flora and fauna. The best way to explore Madu Ganga is by taking a boat safari. Local guides navigate through narrow channels bordered by towering mangroves, where you can spot various bird species, monkeys, and sometimes crocodiles basking in the sun. Along the banks of Madu Ganga, you'll encounter traditional rural life. Visit cinnamon plantations and see how cinnamon is harvested and processed. You can also explore small Buddhist temples nestled amidst the lush greenery, offering insights into local religious practices.
